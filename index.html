About Nikola Tesla

Nikola Tesla was a Serbian-American inventor and electrical engineer whose development of alternating current (AC) generation and transmission enabled the long-distance power grids we use today. He filed hundreds of patents across motors, transformers, radio, X-rays, lighting, and wireless power.

Major Inventions & Ideas

Alternating Current (AC)

Polyphase systems enabling efficient power transmission and distribution.

Generators • Transformers • Long-distance grid

Induction Motor

Self-starting motor using a rotating magnetic field — a workhorse of industry.

Three-phase • High reliability • Low maintenance

Tesla Coil & Resonance

High-voltage transformer demonstrating resonance, wireless energy, and RF effects.

Spark gaps • Resonant LC • High frequency

Radio & Remote Control

Early radio transmission demonstrations and the first radio-controlled boat (1898).

RF oscillators • Tuning • Control by waves

Hydropower at Niagara

AC generation used to light cities and power industry — a historic milestone.

Turbines • Generators • Transmission

Wardenclyffe Vision

Ambitious plan for global wireless telegraphy and power (never completed).

Long-range communication • Resonant earth
