The Big Bang

A Journey Through 13.8 Billion Years

Physical: We are about to witness the most extraordinary event in cosmic history - the birth of space, time, matter, and energy itself.

Philosophical: Before this moment, there was no "before." We stand at the threshold of existence itself, where physics meets metaphysics.

Planck Epoch

0 to 10⁻⁴³ seconds

Temperature: 10³² K Size: 10⁻³⁵ meters

Physical: All four fundamental forces are unified. Quantum gravity dominates. Space and time themselves are quantized and uncertain.

Philosophical: This is the realm of pure potential - where the very concepts of existence and non-existence blur into quantum uncertainty.

Grand Unification Epoch

10⁻⁴³ to 10⁻³⁶ seconds

Temperature: 10²⁸ K Forces: 3 unified

Physical: Gravity separates from the other forces. The strong, weak, and electromagnetic forces remain unified as the electronuclear force.

Philosophical: The first great symmetry breaking - the universe begins to differentiate, choosing specific paths from infinite possibilities.

Cosmic Inflation

10⁻³⁶ to 10⁻³² seconds

Expansion: 10²⁶ times Speed: Faster than light

Physical: Exponential expansion driven by the inflaton field. Space itself expands faster than light, stretching quantum fluctuations to cosmic scales.

Philosophical: The universe makes its first great leap of faith - expanding into the unknown, creating the stage for all future complexity.

Electroweak Epoch

10⁻³² to 10⁻¹² seconds

Temperature: 10¹⁵ K Forces: 2 + 2

Physical: The strong force separates. Electromagnetic and weak forces remain unified. W and Z bosons are massless.

Philosophical: Further differentiation emerges - the universe continues to break symmetries, choosing specific laws from the realm of possibilities.

Quark Epoch

10⁻¹² to 10⁻⁶ seconds

Temperature: 10¹² K State: Quark-gluon plasma

Physical: All four forces are now distinct. Quarks and gluons exist freely in a hot, dense plasma. The Higgs field gives particles mass.

Philosophical: The fundamental building blocks of matter emerge - the universe begins to write the alphabet from which all future complexity will be spelled.

Hadron Epoch

10⁻⁶ to 1 second

Temperature: 10¹⁰ K Formation: Protons & Neutrons

Physical: Quarks combine to form hadrons - protons and neutrons. The strong force confines quarks permanently within these particles.

Philosophical: The first stable structures emerge - the universe learns to build, creating lasting forms from fleeting interactions.

Lepton Epoch

1 to 10 seconds

Temperature: 10⁹ K Particles: Electrons, Neutrinos

Physical: Leptons (electrons, muons, neutrinos) dominate the universe's mass-energy. Most hadrons have annihilated, leaving a small excess of matter.

Philosophical: The great asymmetry reveals itself - why does anything exist at all? The universe's preference for matter over antimatter becomes apparent.

Big Bang Nucleosynthesis

10 seconds to 20 minutes

Temperature: 10⁸ K Elements: H, He, Li

Physical: Protons and neutrons fuse to form the first atomic nuclei. About 75% hydrogen and 25% helium are created, with traces of lithium.

Philosophical: The universe begins its first act of creation - forging the elements that will one day become stars, planets, and life itself.

Photon Epoch

3 minutes to 380,000 years

Temperature: 10⁴ K State: Plasma

Physical: The universe is dominated by photons. Matter exists as a hot plasma of nuclei and free electrons. Light cannot travel far due to constant scattering.

Philosophical: The universe enters its "dark age" - opaque to light, yet pregnant with potential. Like a cosmic cocoon, transformation happens in darkness.

Recombination Era

380,000 years

Temperature: 3,000 K Event: First atoms form

Physical: Electrons combine with nuclei to form the first neutral atoms. The universe becomes transparent to light. The cosmic microwave background is released.

Philosophical: "Let there be light!" The universe opens its eyes for the first time, and light begins its eternal journey through space.

Cosmic Dark Ages

380,000 to 150 million years

Temperature: 100 K State: Neutral gas

Physical: No stars exist yet. The universe is filled with neutral hydrogen and helium gas. Dark matter begins to clump, forming the scaffolding for future structures.

Philosophical: In the darkness, the seeds of complexity are planted. Sometimes the most profound changes happen in silence and shadow.

First Stars (Population III)

150 million to 1 billion years

Mass: 100-1000 solar masses Composition: Pure H & He

Physical: Massive, metal-free stars ignite nuclear fusion. They burn bright and die young, creating the first heavy elements in their cores and explosive deaths.

Philosophical: The universe lights its first candles against the darkness. In their brief, brilliant lives, these stellar giants sacrifice themselves to create the elements of complexity.

Galaxy Formation

1 to 3 billion years

Structure: Dark matter halos Stars: Population II

Physical: Dark matter halos collapse and merge. Gas falls into these wells, forming the first galaxies. Star formation accelerates with metal-enriched gas.

Philosophical: The universe builds its first cities - vast communities of stars bound by gravity and illuminated by nuclear fire. Cosmic civilization begins.

Solar System Formation

4.6 billion years ago

Location: Milky Way Process: Accretion disk

Physical: A molecular cloud collapses to form our Sun and solar system. Planets accrete from the protoplanetary disk through gravitational attraction and collisions.

Philosophical: In one small corner of the cosmos, the stage is set for the next great chapter - the emergence of life, consciousness, and the universe becoming aware of itself.
